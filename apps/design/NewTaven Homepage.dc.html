02 // The problem

Small funds run on memory and spreadsheets.

01

Capital calls live in your inbox.

Amounts, wire dates, and confirmations scattered across threads. No single view of who has funded and who hasn't.

02

LP records drift out of date.

A commitment changes, a contact leaves, an entity is restructured — and the spreadsheet quietly stops matching reality.

03

Follow-ups depend on remembering.

The polite second nudge is the one that gets the wire in. It is also the first thing to fall off a busy week.

03 // How it works

Three steps, then it runs itself.

Bring your fund in once. From there, NewTaven handles the mechanics you would otherwise do by hand.

STEP 01

Bring your LPs in

Import commitments, contacts, and entities. NewTaven becomes the register of record for the whole fund.

STEP 02

Issue a call

Draft a capital call once. Each LP receives their exact figure, wire details, and due date — no mail merge.

STEP 03

Let the cadence run

Wires reconcile against the register. Anyone outstanding gets a measured follow-up on schedule — in your voice.

04 // The cadence

The follow-ups you'd never get around to.

When a wire is still outstanding, NewTaven sends a measured reminder — at 72 hours, 7 days, and 14 days. Considerate, never pushy. You approve the voice once.

05 // Features

Everything the fund needs. Nothing it doesn't.

LP register

A single record of every commitment, contact, and entity — always current, always exportable.

Capital calls & distributions

Issue a call once; each LP gets their exact figure and wire instructions. Distributions run the same way, in reverse.

Automated cadence

Measured follow-ups on outstanding wires — 72h, 7d, 14d — sent in your fund's voice, only until the wire clears.

Investor portal

Each LP sees their commitments, calls, distributions, and documents — without another login you have to support.

Quarterly letters

Compose the letter once; NewTaven personalizes and delivers it on the fifteenth, with read receipts by LP.

Wire reconciliation

Incoming wires match against the register automatically. What clears is marked funded; what doesn't enters the cadence.
