Investor portal · iOS concept

A portfolio in your pocket.

An iOS companion to the NewTaven limited-partner portal. It carries the same restraint the web experience does — hairline rules instead of shadows, square corners, a serif that behaves like a private bank statement — into the places an LP actually reaches for a phone: reviewing a drawdown notice on the move, checking a distribution has landed, reading the quarterly letter in a waiting room.

01
Numbers do the talking
Near-clinical data screens. Chrome stays out of the way; tabular figures line up to the pixel.

Square, not soft
The device rounds its own corners. The app keeps NewTaven's square cards and hairline rules.

Serif for the sums

Brass, sparingly
One warm accent — eyebrows, the quarterly-letter dot, an amount that's due. Never a button.

Access

Biometric entry. No passwords typed on a phone — the portal defers to Face ID and the device passkey.

Portfolio

The consolidated position across every firm and fund — then one fund in full. Figures are the LP's share.

Capital activity

The two things an LP opens a phone for: a drawdown that's due, and a distribution that's landed.

Correspondence & account

Quarterly letters and statements read like documents, not feed cards. The account tab stays deliberately plain.

Notes for build

Data

Every screen maps to an existing endpoint the web portal already calls — /investor/dashboard/overview, /investor/commitments, /investor/capital-calls, /investor/distributions, /investor/communications. No new backend surface required.

Boundaries

The app is read-and-review. It surfaces wire instructions and PDF notices but never initiates a transfer, changes access, or captures a payment — consistent with the portal's contact-link access model.

Fidelity to the system

Cormorant Garamond for sums and letters, Inter Tight for data, Lucide at 1.5px, hairline rules over shadows, square corners, one brass accent. Restraint is the brand — the phone should feel like the same firm.
